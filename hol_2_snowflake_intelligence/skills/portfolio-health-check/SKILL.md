# Portfolio Health Check

When a user asks about overall portfolio health, requests a status update, or wants an executive summary, follow this multi-step analysis framework.

## Step 1: Key Performance Indicators

Query and present these top-line metrics:
- Total portfolio value (sum of all loan amounts)
- Total active customers
- Overall approval rate (current month vs trailing 12-month average)
- Overall delinquency rate (% of loans with late payments)
- Average payment health score across the portfolio

Present these as a quick KPI summary before diving deeper.

## Step 2: Trend Analysis

Look at the last 6-12 months of portfolio data and identify:
- Is application volume trending up, down, or stable?
- Is the approval rate changing?
- Is the delinquency rate improving or worsening?
- Any notable month-over-month shifts?

Describe the direction clearly: "Application volume has been increasing 5% month-over-month since Q3" is better than just showing numbers.

## Step 3: Concentration Risk

Identify where the portfolio is concentrated:
- Top 3 industries by exposure - what % of the total portfolio do they represent?
- Top state by exposure
- Any single customer representing more than 5% of total exposure?
- Loan product mix - is it diversified or concentrated in one product?

Flag any concentration that exceeds 30% in a single segment.

## Step 4: Problem Areas

Identify the weak spots:
- Which industry has the highest delinquency rate?
- Which loan product is underperforming?
- How many loans have a payment health score below 50?
- Are there customers with multiple high-risk loans?

## Step 5: Structure the Executive Summary

Present the final output as:

### Portfolio Health Check - [Current Month]

**Overall Status:** [Healthy / Watch / Concern]

**KPIs at a Glance:**
| Metric | Value | Trend |
|--------|-------|-------|
| (filled from Step 1) |

**Key Observations:**
1. [Most important finding]
2. [Second finding]
3. [Third finding]

**Risks to Monitor:**
- [Risk 1 with specific numbers]
- [Risk 2]

**Recommendation:**
- [One actionable next step]

## Tone and Style

- Lead with the conclusion, then support with data
- Use directional language (improving, declining, stable)
- Round to sensible precision ($1.5M not $1,487,293.42)
- Be concise - this is for executives who have 2 minutes
